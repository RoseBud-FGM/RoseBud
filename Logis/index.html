About Us

We at team Putrid Cookies are a group of highly motivated students from India working tirelessly towards eradicating Female Genital Mutilation(FGM) and ensuring a safer and healthier future for all girls and women through our app "RoseBud".

  • Our Team

    Triptanjana Sarangi, Rajdeep Ghosh, Sayak Ghosh, Rajdeep Chatterjee

  • Our Aim

    Our aim is to raise awareness about the harms of FGM/C and encourage reporting of incidents to empower girls and women, promote their health and well-being, and ultimately end the practice within our community.

  • Our Success

    Success in our FGM/C project should be measured by a significant increase in community knowledge and understanding about the harms of FGM/C, increased reporting of incidents, and ultimately a decrease in the prevalence of the practice within our community.

Our Services

Our Services

Reporting

Our FGM/C project helps in reporting by providing safe and confidential channels for community members to report incidents of the practice, and by empowering girls and women with the knowledge and resources to speak out against FGM/C and seek support from relevant authorities.

Counselling

We help in counseling by providing access to trained professionals who can offer psychological and emotional support to survivors and their families, and by promoting community-based approaches that prioritize the health and well-being of girls and women affected by FGM/C.

Forum

A forum can help by providing a safe space for community members to engage in open and honest discussions about FGM/C, share personal experiences and knowledge, and collaborate on strategies to prevent and respond to incidents of the practice. A forum can also help to build solidarity and promote community-driven solutions that prioritize the health and well-being of girls and women.

Call To Action

We must take action to end the harmful practice of FGM/C. This includes educating ourselves and others about the physical and psychological harms of the practice, promoting alternative cultural practices that value the rights and autonomy of girls and women, and reporting incidents to empower survivors and prevent future occurrences. Together, we can create a world where every girl and woman is safe and free from harm.

No Time For Inaction: Female Genital Mutilation Is Global, But So Is The Movement To End It

An article by Hindustan Times

According to official UNICEF figures, female genital mutilation (FGM) affects at least 200 million women and girls in 31 countries worldwide. We know these numbers are a woeful underrepresentation, as there are at least 60 other countries where the practice has been documented but no national-level data is available. Eradicating female genital mutilation (FGM) worldwide by 2030 is one of the commitments that all 193 UN member states agreed to when they signed onto the Sustainable Development Goals in 2015. Despite overwhelming evidence that female genital mutilation occurs in at least 90 countries, the efforts of the UNFPA-UNICEF Joint Programme to Eliminate Female Genital Mutilation the largest global program to accelerate the elimination of FGM are focused on just 17 countries. The Joint Programme targets Burkina Faso, Djibouti, Egypt, Eritrea, Ethiopia, Gambia, Guinea, Guinea-Bissau, Kenya, Mali, Mauritania, Nigeria, Senegal, Sudan, Somalia, Uganda, and Yemen; this goes to show that there is a need for further investment and increased political will by all key actors including States to ensure that the practice is eliminated across all countries where it is practiced.
